HAMBURGER OF THE WEEK

Time

  • Level: Easy
  • Prep: 20 Min
  • Yeild: 4 Hamburgers
  • Cook: 15 min
  • Total: 25 min

Bengal Burger

  • 25 Reviews
"No more dry, lackluster burgers. These are juicy, and spices can easily added or changed to suit anyone's taste. Baste frequently with your favorite barbeque sauce. If you find the meat misture too mushy, just add more bread crmbs until if forms patties that hold their shape."
Ingredients
  • 24 ounces fround chuck
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Spice Mix, optional, recipe follows
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 4 slices cheese, such as Cheddar, American cheese
  • 4 soft hamburger-style buns, split
  • 4 slices beefsteak tomatoes, optional
  • Assorted lettuces and greens, such as iceberg
  • Assorted mustards, optional
  • Mayonnaise, optional
  • Ketchup, optional
Spice mix
  • 1 ttablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 teaspoons garlic powder
  • 2 teaspoons onion powder
  • Hot Sauce, to taste
  1. Preheat the oven to 450 degrees F. Place a roasting rack on a foil-lined sheet in the oven.
  2. Using your hands, break the meat into small pices and comb evenly but loosely on parchment or waxed paper-lined baking sheet. Spread the meat out and season it generously with salt and pepper. If desired, add the spice mix at this time.
  3. Divide the meat into 4 portions (about 6 ounces each). Using your hands, form each portion into a ball-shape by gently tossing it from 1 hand to the other. (Don't over work or press to firmly on the meat.) Gently form each portion into a patty about 3 1/2 inches wide and 1-inch think.
  4. Preheat a large cast iron skillet over medium-low heat for 5 minutes. Raise the heat to high and add the oil. Add the patties and cook, turning once, until well-browned, about 2 minutes each side.
  5. Using a spatula, transfer the bam burgers to the roasting rack in the oven and coninue to desired doneness, 8 to 9 minutes for medium-rare, 10-11 minutes for medium, and 13-15 for well-done. If you are using cheese, top the hamburgers during thier last couple of minutes of cooking to melt.
  6. Transfer the hamburgers to a plate, let rest for a couple minutes before serving. Meanwhile, toast the hamburger buns. Assemble the hamburgers with the condiments and toppings of your choice. Serve.
  7. When cooking on a countertop grill, turn on grill, then place burgers on the heated grill.
  8. Timing for a countertop grill: For Medium-Rare: Cook for 3 minutes covered and then unplug the machine and continue to cook covered for 2 to 3 more minutes.
  9. For Medium: Cook for 4 minutes covered and then unplug the machine and continue to cook covered for 4 more minutes. 0.
  10. For Well: Cook for 5 minutes covered and then unplug the machine CB
